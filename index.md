I’m a PhD student with an MSc in Theoretical and Computational Methods from the University of Helsinki, where I also worked as a research assistant in the Sum of Products group. During my master’s, I focused on developing statistical and computational methods for machine learning, with an emphasis on building general tools for understanding and modeling data.

Alongside academia, I have about 10 years of experience as a software engineer. Most recently, I led the technical side of a game development project at Netflix. That mix of research and engineering still shapes how I work: I like building things that combine deep computation with something people can actually use, from research simulations to interactive applications.

## About

My current work sits between molecular simulation, statistical modeling, and scientific computing. I’m especially interested in methods that are both mathematically grounded and practical for real research workflows. This site collects papers, code, and research artifacts in one place so they remain easy to browse and maintain.

## Lab

I am currently part of the [Lab for Computational Molecular Engineering](https://mendelsgroup.net.technion.ac.il/), where the research centers on computational approaches to molecular design, soft matter, and complex biological and chemical systems. The lab combines simulation, theory, and data-driven methods to study how molecular-level interactions give rise to larger-scale behavior.

What I value about the group is that it treats computation as both a scientific lens and an engineering discipline. That makes it a strong environment for developing new methods while staying close to substantive problems in molecular science.

## Open Source

I also contribute to [lammps.js](https://github.com/lammps/lammps.js), an open-source project that brings LAMMPS to the browser through WebAssembly-based tooling. It is a good example of the kind of work I enjoy: making advanced computational methods more accessible, interactive, and usable in practice.
